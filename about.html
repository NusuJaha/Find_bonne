ABOUT US

Bonne means caring, supportive and delightful. Bonne is a web platform to help people, specially employed women to foster their children with proper care and medication. So, we will build a system where people will get adequate and trustworthy babysitters for their children whom they can trust indiscreetly.

Since the rate of the birth of the children are increasing day by day, the number of working women in decreasing. From the perspective of the Bangladesh and any other developing countries, this is necessary for most parents because many families require two incomes to get by financially.

To resolve all the given problems, we want to implement the service “Bonne". Bonne is necessary for our society To ensure the parents that, their kids are in good hands, To encourage women in job sectors, To ensure safety of the children, and To create a platform for earning money for the university students or any other people who are qualified enough to take proper care of a child. Bonne is also important for academic advancement, learning the interaction with other adults, smoother transition to Kindergarten, moreover social and economic benefits for parents.

Therefore, first we will construct a platform where people from all over the country can register themselves first. Some as guardians, the others as benefactor. This will be a quite lengthy procedure for registration. We might ask for their NID, Passport or Birth Certificate, NOC, or sometimes Police clearance to verify them. For security purpose, we will collect data and submit to the respective police stations so that no further occurrences held. After the registration process, We can assign one bonne to one child on the guardian’s preferred time. We will also have few rules and regulations for the workers. They are also required to show updates to the guardians through text messages or giving snaps of the child.

For one hour every bonne will be charged 150 BDT initially. We might give few voucher code in the beginning so that the gurdians can understand the essence.

This will ultimately help the guardians to ensure the safety of the child, as well as a source to earn money for the youths on their leisure time. Overall, Bonne has a great socio-economic and academic impact on our society.

Dr. Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan

Dr. Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan is basically the captain of the ship, An young, charismatic and idealistic person who advised his students to implement the project and helped every possible way.

Dr. Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan is currently working as an Associate Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at North South University, Dhaka, Bangladesh. He received the B.Eng. degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Queen Mary University of London (QMUL), United Kingdom, with First Class Honours. Dr Khan received the Draper’s Company Undergraduate Prize 2008 from Queen Mary University of London for outstanding academic merit. He completed his PhD degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Queen Mary University of London (QMUL). His PhD was funded by Queen Mary University of London full scholarship. After completing his PhD, Dr Khan worked as a postdoctoral research assistant under Engineering and Physical Science Research Council (EPSRC) funded IMPACT QM KTA Scheme 1 project in the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science at Queen Mary University of London, UK.

Dr. Khan received Dr Fatema Rashid first best paper award in the International Conference on Advances in Electrical Engineering (ICAEE 2013). He also received best presenter award in the 3rd International Conference on Informatics, Electronics, and VisionTechnology, (ICIEV-2014). Dr Khan received best poster paper award in the 1st International Conference on Electrical Engineering and Information & Communication Technology (ICEICT-2014). In addition, he received best paper award in the 10th Global Engineering, Science and Technology Conference, 2015.

His research interests include wearable antennas and radios, small compact, smart and multiband antennas, body-centric wireless communications, electromagnetism, cognitive radio, electrically small antennas, smart cooperative network: personal and body area network, radio channel modelling, remote healthcare, wireless sensor network, UWB antennas and system.

Dr. Khan has authored and co-authored more than 60 peer-reviewed leading journals and international conferences. He is an active reviewer of IEEE Transactions on Antennas and Propagations, IEEE Magazine on Antenna and Propagation, IEEE Antenna and Wireless Propagation Letter, IEEE Communication Letter, IET Microwaves Antennas & Propagation, Wireless Personal Communications, Springer, International Journal on Communications Antenna and Propagation, International Journal of Microwave and Wireless Technologies and numerous IEEE Conferences.
